How to Solve a Rubik's Cube (Beginner Method)

This beginner-friendly guide teaches you how to solve a Rubik's Cube using J Perm's method. Each step is broken down clearly so you can follow along and understand why each move matters.

Step 1: Make the White Cross

Create a plus sign on the white face. Make sure the side colors of each white edge also match the center piece of the adjacent sides. This helps align the whole cube properly for the next steps.

Step 2: Solve the White Corners

Insert each white corner piece into the bottom layer so the entire white face is complete. Use the move: R' D' R D repeatedly until each corner is in place. Rotate the bottom layer (D) and repeat for each corner.

Step 3: Solve the Middle Layer Edges

Look for non-yellow edge pieces in the top layer and align them with the correct center. Use:

  • If it needs to go left: U' L' U L U F U' F'
  • If it needs to go right: U R U' R' U' F' U F

Repeat until the middle layer is filled correctly.

Step 4: Make the Yellow Cross

Hold the yellow face on top. Use the move F R U R' U' F' to go from a dot → L-shape → line → full yellow cross.

Step 5: Position Yellow Corners

Find a yellow corner in the correct position. If one is right, keep it front-right and use: U R U' L' U R' U' L. If none are correct, do the move once and then find the correct corner.

Step 6: Rotate Yellow Corners

Use R' D' R D repeatedly to twist each corner so yellow is on top. Once they are all yellow, rotate only the top layer to finish the cube!
